# Emergency Contacts & Resources

*Last Updated: July 23, 2026, 09:22 PM*

If you or anyone you know has been injured, detained, or needs immediate assistance following the Jantar Mantar incident, please refer to the resources below.

## 1. Medical Assistance & First Aid
- **General Emergency Medical Services:** Dial `102` or `108`
- **Nearby Hospitals (Central Delhi):**
  - **Ram Manohar Lohia (RML) Hospital:** Baba Kharak Singh Road, Connaught Place. Emergency: `011-23365525`
  - **Lok Nayak Jai Prakash (LNJP) Hospital:** Jawaharlal Nehru Marg. Emergency: `011-23232400`
  - **Lady Hardinge Medical College (LHMC):** Connaught Place (especially for women and children). Emergency: `011-23363777`
- **Protest Volunteer Medical Desks:** (Contact local volunteer coordinators for location of active first-aid setups).

## 2. Legal Aid & Detainee Support
- **Human Rights Law Network (HRLN):** Legal representation and advice for detained peaceful protesters.
  - Hotline/Office: `011-24374501`
- **Association for Protection of Democratic Rights (APDR) / Lawyers for Justice:**
  - Standard contact info for legal representation during protests.
- **What to do if detained:**
  - Ask for the grounds of arrest immediately.
  - Female detainees must be arrested/accompanied by female police officers and cannot be detained after sunset or before sunrise except in exceptional circumstances with judicial permission.
  - Protesters have the right to contact a legal representative immediately.

## 3. Human Rights Organizations & Media Helplines
- **National Human Rights Commission (NHRC) helpline:** Dial `14433`
- **Alternative News Desk / Independent Media:** To submit footage of the lathi charge or student testimonies, contact verified independent journalists or upload files directly to the `media/` folder in this archive.
