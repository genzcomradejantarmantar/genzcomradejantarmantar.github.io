The Demand is Unchanged: Why Students Rejected Closed-Door Talks

Following the intense crackdown on July 20, thousands of students re-assembled at Jantar Mantar on the morning of July 21, 2026. The atmosphere was filled with resolve.

In the afternoon, the government offered to hold talks. Cockroach Janta Party (CJP) student representatives Saurav Das and Ashutosh Ranka met with Union Minister JP Nadda to hand over our list of demands, including the non-negotiable resignation of Education Minister Dharmendra Pradhan.

Why We Reject Private Dialogues

Following the initial meeting, the government invited representatives to hold further discussions at ministerial offices. However, the student leadership officially turned down this offer for closed-door negotiations.

The reasons for this decision are clear:

  • Transparency: Closed-door negotiations allow authorities to dilute student demands away from public scrutiny. We demand that all negotiations happen in a public forum at Jantar Mantar or an open, neutral venue.
  • Accountability: The future of lakhs of students cannot be decided behind closed doors. Every stakeholder has a right to know what is being offered and discussed.
  • Dignity: We will not hold private negotiations with a government that used batons on students asking for fair exams just 24 hours prior.

Our core demand remains unchanged: the resignation of the Union Education Minister, an independent judicial probe into the paper leak, and comprehensive structural reform of the competitive exam system. We urge all students to maintain solidarity and continue peaceful mobilization.
