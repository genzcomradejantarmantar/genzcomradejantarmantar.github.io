Police Crackdown on 'Chalo Sansad' March: Eyewitness Reports

On July 20, 2026, thousands of NEET aspirants, university students, and youth activists assembled peacefully at Jantar Mantar to demand accountability for the NEET-UG paper leak. What began as a peaceful assembly turned into a major confrontation when students initiated the "Chalo Sansad" (March to Parliament) rally around 11:30 AM.

As the march commnced, heavy police forces barricaded the routes near Raisina Road, Shastri Bhawan, and Parliament Street. In the afternoon, without any advance warning, security forces launched a sudden lathi-charge and deployed tear gas to disperse the students.

Figure 1: Representation of the twilight student assembly at Jantar Mantar prior to the police crackdown.

Injuries and Detentions

According to student volunteers and medical first-aid desks, over 170 people sustained injuries during the crackdown. This included head injuries and fractures from batons, as well as breathing difficulties from tear gas. Shockingly, the police also dismantled medical aid tents at Jantar Mantar and shoved volunteer doctors attempting to provide medical assistance.

Additionally, over 90 students were detained across five police stations in central Delhi. Cellphone network signals in the Jantar Mantar area were temporarily throttled to prevent students from sharing live video feeds.

This crackdown has failed to silence the students. In fact, it has only intensified the movement, with more students joining the sit-in at Jantar Mantar.
